# Fidelity Wise Origin Bitcoin Fund — 2025 Grantor Trust Tax Reporting Statement

- **EIN:** 86-6606379
- **CUSIP:** 315948109
- **Ticker:** FBTC

The following information is being provided to assist shareholders of the Fidelity Wise Origin Bitcoin Fund (FBTC) with reporting of their U.S. federal taxable income and expenses for the period January 1, 2025 through December 31, 2025, pursuant to Treas. Reg. Sec. 1.671-5. Under this regulation, the Fidelity Wise Origin Bitcoin Fund is considered a widely held fixed investment trust.

The example provided herein is based on a U.S. individual shareholder who purchased their Fidelity Wise Origin Bitcoin Fund shares for cash. All shareholders should contact their own tax advisors as to the tax consequences of the information reported herein for Fidelity Wise Origin Bitcoin Fund.

## Taxation of FBTC

Fidelity Wise Origin Bitcoin Fund is intended to be a grantor trust for U.S. federal income tax purposes. As a result, FBTC itself should not be subject to U.S. federal income tax. Instead, FBTC's income and expenses should "flow through" to the Shareholders.

## Taxation of U.S. Individual Shareholders

Each shareholder will be treated, for U.S. federal income tax purposes, as if they directly owned a pro rata share of the underlying assets held in FBTC. A shareholder also will be treated as if they directly received their respective pro rata share of FBTC's income, if any, and as if they directly incurred their respective pro rata share of FBTC's expenses, subject to some specialized allocation rules for widely held fixed investment trusts ("WHFITs"). Shareholders should contact their own tax advisors as to the state and local tax consequences.

FBTC holds only Bitcoin and, accordingly, received no income during the year.

FBTC expenses, which are mostly expected to be miscellaneous itemized deductions for shareholders, are generally not deductible for U.S. federal income tax purposes by individuals in 2025. However, the sale of Bitcoin to cover FBTC expenses is used to calculate each shareholder's tax basis.

Because FBTC sold only a de minimis amount of Bitcoin for payment of FBTC's expenses during the year and made no distributions of sale proceeds to its shareholders, under Treas. Reg. Sec. 1.671-5(c)(2)(iv)(B) FBTC is not required to report the gross proceeds of FBTC's sales to its shareholders.

The information contained in the 2025 Gross Proceeds File along with the provided examples will allow a shareholder to calculate gain or loss related to the de minimis sale of Bitcoin in 2025.

> **Note:** The 2025 Gross Proceeds File daily table is omitted from this document. It is available in the [original PDF](https://www.fidelity.com/bin-public/060_www_fidelity_com/documents/research/1185828.2.0_WHFIT%20Annual%20Stmt%20UDA_FBTC_2025.pdf).

## Gross Proceeds File — Column Descriptions

The Fidelity Wise Origin Bitcoin Fund 2025 Gross Proceeds File shows the number of Bitcoin acquired per FBTC share purchased, number of Bitcoin sold per FBTC share purchased, and proceeds per FBTC share purchased. You can use this information to calculate your gain or loss on sale of Bitcoin to pay FBTC expenses.

Please note that most FBTC expenses are expected to be miscellaneous itemized deductions, which are generally not deductible by individuals for U.S. federal income tax purposes in year 2025.

The columns are:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Date | Calendar date |
| Bitcoin Per Share | Number of Bitcoin each FBTC share represents on that date |
| Per Share Bitcoin Sold To Cover FBTC Expenses | Bitcoin sold per share on that date to cover fund expenses (appears on month-end rows) |
| Proceeds Per Share (USD) to Cover FBTC Expenses | USD proceeds per share from Bitcoin sold to cover expenses (appears on month-end rows) |

## Example Calculation

The example below illustrates the calculation of a shareholder's investment expenses from FBTC and gain (loss) from the sale of Bitcoin to cover expenses for year 2025, as well as the adjusted tax cost basis of Bitcoin held.

**Scenario:** Shareholder ABC purchases 1,000 FBTC shares on 9/9/2025 at a price of $98.50 per share for a total purchase price of $98,500. Shareholder ABC had no other purchases or sales of FBTC shares during 2025.

### Calculation of Investment Expense for Year 2025

For shares purchased on 9/9/2025:

| Item | Value | Variable |
|------|------:|:--------:|
| Days held by ABC in September 2025 | 21 | a |
| Total days in September 2025 | 30 | b |
| Proceeds Per Share (USD) to cover FBTC Expenses for September (from Gross Proceeds File, 09/30/2025) | 0.02072663 | c |
| Expense per Share (September 2025) | 0.01450864 | d = a/b × c |
| Expense per Share for October–December 2025 (sum of monthly "Proceeds Per Share (USD) to Cover FBTC Expense" for Oct, Nov, Dec) | 0.05251118 | e |
| **Total Expense per share for year 2025** | **0.06701982** | **x = d + e** |
| Number of Shares Held | 1,000 | y |
| **Total Investment Expense for year 2025** | **$67.02** | **z = x × y** |

### Calculation of Gain (Loss) and Cost Basis

> Shareholders with several purchases should calculate investment expense, gain, loss and adjusted basis separately for each lot and then sum up the results of each lot to arrive at the net capital gain or loss.

#### Step 1: Identify the shareholder's pro rata ownership of Bitcoin

In the column labeled "Bitcoin Per Share" of the Gross Proceeds File, identify the number of Bitcoin (i.e. 0.00087206 Bitcoin per share) each FBTC share represents on 9/9/2025 (Shareholder ABC's purchase date).

For Shareholder ABC, the total number of Bitcoin acquired for its 9/9/2025 purchase is:

- 0.00087206 Bitcoin/share × 1,000 shares = **0.87206 Bitcoin**

#### Step 2: Calculate the number of Bitcoin sold during 2025 to pay expenses

The number of Bitcoin sold per share during 2025 includes the prorated number of Bitcoin sold from purchase date of 9/9/2025 through the next sale date (9/30/2025) and all subsequent sales of Bitcoin through year end.

| Item | Value | Variable |
|------|------:|:--------:|
| Days held by ABC in September 2025 | 21 | a |
| Total days in September 2025 | 30 | b |
| Bitcoin Sold To Cover FBTC Expenses (September 2025) | 0.00000018 | m |
| Bitcoin sold per share (September 2025) | 0.00000013 | n = a/b × m |
| Bitcoin sold from October–December 2025 (sum of monthly "Per Share Bitcoin Sold To Cover FBTC Expenses" for Oct, Nov, Dec) | 0.00000054 | o |
| **Total Bitcoin sold per share for year 2025** | **0.00000067** | **p = o + n** |
| Number of Shares Held | 1,000 | y |
| **Total Bitcoin sold for year 2025 to cover expense** | **0.00067000** | **q = p × y** |

#### Step 3: Calculate cost of Bitcoin sold from shareholder's account

| Item | Value |
|------|------:|
| Total Bitcoin sold (Step 2) | 0.00067 |
| Divided by: Number of Bitcoin acquired (Step 1) | 0.87206 |
| Times: Purchase Price | $98,500 |
| **Cost basis of Bitcoin sold** | **$75.68** |

#### Step 4: Calculate shareholder's investment expenses

(Same calculation as the Investment Expense table above — result: **$67.02**)

#### Step 5: Calculate shareholder's gain or loss on sales of Bitcoin

Gain or loss is determined by subtracting Shareholder ABC's cost of Bitcoin sold from total proceeds. Shareholder ABC's proceeds are equal to expenses as calculated in Step 4.

| Item | Value |
|------|------:|
| Total proceeds (same as investment expense from Step 4) | $67.02 |
| Less: Total cost of Bitcoin sold (Step 3) | $75.68 |
| **Total Reportable Gain (Loss)** | **($8.65)** |

#### Step 6: Calculate shareholder's adjusted Bitcoin held and cost basis

| Item | Value |
|------|------:|
| Number of Bitcoin purchased on 9/9/2025 (Step 1) | 0.87206 |
| Less: Bitcoin sold during 2025 (Step 2) | 0.00067 |
| **Adjusted number of Bitcoin at 12/31/2025** | **0.87139** |
| Original purchase cost on 9/9/2025 | $98,500.00 |
| Less: Cost of Bitcoin sold during 2025 (Step 3) | $75.68 |
| **Adjusted shareholder's basis at 12/31/2025** | **$98,424.32** |

Shareholder ABC's adjusted cost and number of Bitcoin calculated in Step 6 are ABC's adjusted number of Bitcoin and adjusted basis for the 9/9/2025 purchase at the end of 2025.

## Additional Notes

- Shareholders with several purchases should calculate investment expense, gain, loss and adjusted basis separately for each lot and then sum up the results of each lot to arrive at the net capital gain or loss.
- For shareholders who purchased their shares prior to 2025, the adjusted basis and adjusted number of Bitcoin at 12/31/2024 should be used as the beginning cost and beginning number of Bitcoin for 2025 instead of the original purchase cost and original Bitcoin purchased.
- Shareholders with additional purchases in 2025 will have two or more cost lots to account for in 2025. The calculation of gains and losses will be affected by the cost basis method used to account for the sales.
